# Superstore Business Intelligence Analysis

This repository contains a Business Intelligence coursework report analysing the **Sample Superstore** dataset. The project focuses on sales performance, profitability, discounting behaviour, regional performance, product sub-categories, data mining, and data ethics.

The report combines visual business analysis with data mining using **K-Means clustering** to identify patterns in sales, profit, discount levels, regions, and sub-categories.

## Project Overview

The project uses the **Sample Superstore** dataset, a common retail dataset used for business intelligence, data visualisation, and analytics practice.

The analysis investigates why some regions and product categories generate high sales but lower profitability. Special attention is given to the relationship between:

* sales
* profit
* discounts
* regions
* customer segments
* product categories
* sub-categories
* seasonal patterns

## Key Analysis Areas

### Regional Profitability

The report analyses regional differences in sales and profit, with a focus on identifying why some regions have strong sales but weaker profitability.

### Discount Impact

The report investigates how discount levels affect profitability across different sub-categories and regions.

### Product Sub-Category Performance

The analysis identifies underperforming and high-performing product sub-categories, especially where high sales do not translate into strong profit.

### K-Means Clustering

The report applies K-Means clustering across multiple iterations to identify meaningful patterns in the dataset. The clustering process is refined by adjusting the number of clusters and selecting more relevant variables.

### Data Ethics

The report discusses ethical, legal, and professional issues in data analysis, including privacy, transparency, accountability, and bias.

## Main Findings

The report highlights that high sales do not always result in high profitability. In particular, heavy discounting and underperforming product categories can reduce profit margins significantly.

The K-Means clustering analysis provides additional segmentation insights, helping to identify regional and product-level patterns that can support better pricing, discounting, and operational decisions.

---

## Recommendations

The report recommends:

* limiting excessive discounts on low-profit sub-categories
* focusing promotions on high-margin products
* improving regional pricing strategies
* using clustering insights to support more targeted business decisions
* applying ethical principles when interpreting and using business data
